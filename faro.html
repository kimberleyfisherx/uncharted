Faro

Our complete guide to Faro

Faro, the captivating capital city of Portugal's Algarve region, is a destination that seamlessly blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Set along the stunning Ria Formosa lagoon, Faro enchants visitors with its picturesque Old Town, charming cobblestone streets, and ancient city walls. The city's rich history is showcased through architectural gems like Faro Cathedral and the Arco da Vila, while the peaceful Jardim Manuel Bivar provides a serene oasis in the heart of the city. Faro is also a gateway to the breathtaking Ria Formosa Natural Park, a haven for birdwatching and nature enthusiasts. From the bustling marina to the lively markets and traditional eateries serving mouthwatering seafood dishes, Faro delights travelers with its authentic and vibrant ambiance. Whether you're wandering the historic streets, exploring the surrounding natural wonders, or simply savoring the local cuisine, Faro offers a captivating experience that embodies the essence of the Algarve region.

Where to Stay

Hotel Faro & Beach Club

Located in the heart of Faro's historic center, Hotel Faro & Beach Club offers modern and comfortable accommodations with stunning views of the marina and Ria Formosa. The rooftop pool and terrace provide a perfect spot to relax and enjoy panoramic vistas.

Eva Senses Hotel

Situated on Faro's waterfront, Eva Senses Hotel offers contemporary rooms with stylish decor. Its central location allows easy access to Faro's attractions, and the rooftop pool offers a fantastic place to unwind while enjoying the cityscape.

Pousada Palácio de Estói

Located in the nearby village of Estói, this luxury hotel is set in a beautifully restored 19th-century palace. With its opulent interiors, stunning gardens, and panoramic views, Pousada Palácio de Estói offers a truly elegant and memorable experience.

Where to eat and drink

Ria Formosa

Located near the marina, Ria Formosa is a popular restaurant known for its stunning views of the Ria Formosa lagoon. Enjoy a variety of seafood specialties, including grilled octopus and prawns, while taking in the beautiful surroundings.

Faz Gostos

Situated in Faro's historic center, Faz Gostos offers a blend of Portuguese and Mediterranean flavors in a chic and modern setting. With a menu that includes tapas, grilled meats, and innovative dishes, this restaurant is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Arco da Vila

Set within the walls of Faro's Old Town, Arco da Vila is a charming café and wine bar. Indulge in their selection of Portuguese wines and enjoy light bites like cheese platters and tapas. The outdoor seating area provides a lovely ambiance for relaxing and people-watching.

Things to do

Monsaraz

Located in the Alentejo region, the medieval village of Monsaraz offers a glimpse into Portugal's rich history. Explore its narrow streets, visit the 14th-century castle, and take in panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The charming village is known for its tranquil atmosphere and stunning sunsets.

Aveiro

Often referred to as the "Venice of Portugal," Aveiro is a charming coastal city known for its canals, colorful moliceiro boats, and exquisite Art Nouveau architecture. Take a leisurely boat ride along the canals, taste the famous ovos moles (sweet egg pastries), and explore the charming historic center.
