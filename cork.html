Cork

Our complete guide to Cork

Nestled in the picturesque southwestern part of Ireland, Cork is a charming city and county that captivates visitors with its beauty and warmth. Situated along the tranquil banks of the River Lee, it boasts a stunning natural harbor that beckons both locals and tourists alike. As Ireland's second-largest city, Cork exudes a unique blend of vibrant energy and rich history.It proudly serves as the beating heart of County Cork, not only as an administrative hub but also as a cultural epicenter. With its delightful blend of scenic landscapes, welcoming locals, and a wealth of attractions, Cork promises an unforgettable experience for all who venture to its captivating shores.

Where to Stay

Hotel Isaacs Cork City

Hotel Isaacs Cork City, is a charming boutique hotel just a leisurely 5-minute stroll away from the bustling city center. Indulge your taste buds at the critically-acclaimed Greenes Restaurant, where you can savor a unique dining experience overlooking our very own enchanting floodlit waterfall. Nestled under a charming stone archway and surrounded by a delightful courtyard garden, this culinary haven specializes in mouthwatering fish and seafood dishes that showcase the best of Cork's gastronomy. Hotel Isaac's location also makes exploring the city a breeze. Kent Railway Station and Cork Bus Station, conveniently located just a 10-minute walk away, provide easy access to Cork Airport and beyond. Plus, for avid golfers, Hotel Isaacs is perfectly situated to take advantage of the nearby world-class golf courses, including the prestigious Fota Island and Douglas.

Where to eat and drink

Tony's Bistro

Visiting Tony's, a renowned breakfast spot in Cork as part of an Ireland tour, is a delightful culinary experience not to be missed. With a diverse breakfast menu that includes gluten-free options, guests are spoiled for choice. The attentive waitstaff ensures a pleasant dining experience, offering refills on pots of tea and catering to individual preferences. Tony's stands out for its exceptional food quality and reasonable prices. For the adventurous eaters, the "Godfather Breakfast" presents an exciting challenge. The premises exude an inviting ambiance, complete with soothing music, an extensive menu, and a friendly and professional staff.

Arthur Mayne's Pharmacy

Hidden within the vibrant streets of Cork City, Arthur Mayne's Pharmacy stands as a testament to history and transformation. Stepping into this 120-year-old establishment feels like entering a time capsule. Once a bustling chemist, it has now been beautifully repurposed into a charming wine bar and restaurant. The nostalgia-inducing interior, with its preserved wooden cabinets and antique signage, creates an ambiance that transports you to a bygone era. Arthur Mayne's is the perfect destination for an afternoon coffee accompanied by freshly baked scones, or a delightful spot to savor a delicious meal. Their extensive wine selection, sourced locally and globally, caters to all palates. And don't forget to visit the intriguing restrooms adorned with vintage newspaper clippings. Just remember, this enchanting venue is highly sought after, so arrive early to secure a seat and immerse yourself in the captivating allure of Arthur Mayne's Pharmacy-turned-wine bar.

Things to do

Cork's English Market

Experience the timeless charm of Cork's English Market, a true gem that has captivated visitors for over two centuries. Established in 1788, this historic covered market has weathered countless challenges, remaining a beloved part of Cork's retail landscape. Known as the "stomach and soul" of the city, it offers a personal and unique shopping experience, with local, independent food producers and retailers serving up an array of fresh, local, and international delights. Step inside this architectural marvel, soak in its rich heritage, and savor the flavors that have attracted visitors from around the world.

Cork Ghost Tour

Prepare for a side-splitting adventure through the ancient streets of Cork with the "Horrible Hilarious Histories, Tall Tales, Ghastly Ghost Stories & Shenanigans" walking tour. Brace yourself for an unforgettable theatrical experience that combines local ghost stories, dramatic historical accounts, and plenty of laughs along the way. This dynamic and honest tour breathes life into Cork's unique history, leaving no stone unturned. Led by the charismatic Damian, this tour promises to keep you entertained from start to finish. As you stroll through the city's historic landmarks, you'll be regaled with spine-chilling ghost stories that will make your hair stand on end. But fear not, for Damian's comedic flair ensures that every fright is balanced with laughter.
